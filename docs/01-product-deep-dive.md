# Product Deep Dive

## Core Product

HabeshaLocal is a local business discovery and reviews platform for Addis Ababa. It is intended to fill the gap left by incomplete Google Maps data and the lack of a structured Ethiopian alternative to Yelp or TripAdvisor.

The product should feel local in both content and structure:

- Addis neighborhood taxonomy matters.
- Ethiopian dining and lifestyle tags matter.
- ETB-aware pricing matters.
- Trustworthy, moderated community reviews matter.

## Primary Personas

### Consumers

- Browse businesses by category and neighborhood.
- Search for specific use cases such as coffee, dinner, salons, clinics, and hotels.
- Save places for later.
- Read trusted reviews before visiting.

### Business owners

- Claim a listing.
- Maintain business details and media.
- Respond to reviews.
- Monitor visibility and engagement over time.

### Admins

- Approve or reject listings.
- Moderate reviews.
- Review abuse reports.
- Validate ownership claims.

## Public Experience Requirements

- Modern, polished home page with strong editorial feel.
- Fast business discovery with mobile-friendly sorting and filtering.
- High-confidence business detail pages with clear summary metrics.
- Review display that reflects moderation state correctly.
- Reporting flows that feel native to the product, not bolted on.

## High-Value Features

- Hero search and category-led discovery
- Ethiopian neighborhood filters
- Save and unsave behavior
- Business detail page with rich summary information
- Review system with rating, title, body, tags, and moderation state
- Abuse reporting for businesses and reviews
- Claim-your-business funnel
- Admin moderation dashboards

## UX Direction To Preserve

- Premium but approachable visual design
- Editorial landing page composition
- Rounded surfaces, layered depth, and soft gradients
- Strong mobile usability
- Modern, intentional typography rather than default SaaS styling
- A confidence-building trust-and-safety narrative integrated into the product

## MVP Outcome

The MVP should let a new user land on the app, discover businesses quickly, trust the public rating system, save places they like, and understand that moderation and reporting are real parts of the platform.
